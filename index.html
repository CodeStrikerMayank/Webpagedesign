Aerodynamic Profiles

The physical design of a UAV dictates its flight envelope, endurance limitations, and deployment requirements.

FIXED-WING

Rigid airfoil requiring forward momentum to generate lift. High lift-to-drag ratio for sustained cruising.

  • Optimized for: Long-endurance, mapping
  • Launch: Runway / Catapult
  • Optimal Use: HALE missions, ISR

MULTI-ROTOR

Vertical-axis rotors providing VTOL capabilities and localized hovering stability.

  • Optimized for: Localized precision
  • Launch: Vertical Takeoff (VTOL)
  • Optimal Use: Urban tactical, inspection

SINGLE-ROTOR

Primary aerodynamic rotor with tail torque control. Higher efficiency than multi-rotors.

  • Optimized for: Heavy payload, stability
  • Engine: Internal Combustion possible
  • Optimal Use: Logistical transport

HYBRID VTOL

Combines vertical lift motors with a fixed-wing chassis for runway-independent long-range flight.

  • Optimized for: Agile deployment
  • Range: High-speed BVLOS
  • Optimal Use: Expeditionary Recon
